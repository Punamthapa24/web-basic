What is Bootstrap?

Bootstrap is a popular open-source front-end framework used to create responsive and mobile-first websites and web applications. Developed initially by Twitter, it's designed to simplify the web development process by providing pre-designed templates, components, and styles. It is like a ready-made toolkit for building websites. It gives you pre-designed blocks (like buttons, menus, and layouts) and a flexible grid to easily arrange content. Plus, it makes your website look good on phones, tablets, and computers without much extra work. It's like having a shortcut to design a professional-looking site quickly!

Why bootstrap was introduced?

Bootstrap was introduced to solve common challenges web developers faced, such as inconsistent designs and a lack of tools for responsive websites. Before Bootstrap, creating websites that looked good across different devices (like computers, tablets, and phones) was tedious and time-consuming.

  • To ensure websites looked and worked the same in all modern browsers.
